The Science Behind
AquaVision

Underwater images suffer from wavelength-dependent light absorption and scattering — causing colour casts, haze, blur, and low visibility. AquaVision automatically diagnoses and corrects each degradation type.

03 — Architectures & Metrics

Architectures Trained

Three CNNs — Custom CNN, MobileNetV2, and ResNet-50 — were trained and compared on the UIEB benchmark. MobileNetV2 achieved 93% accuracy and is deployed in production. All three architectures are available in the AI Reports page.

Enhancement Strategy

Images: 12-stage adaptive pipeline (dark channel prior, guided filter, multi-scale Laplacian fusion, CLAHE, Lanczos4 upscaling). Video: frame-by-frame temporal OpenCV pipeline with H.264 re-encoding. Both pipelines use per-class parameter calibration.

05 — Project Context

AquaVision was developed as a B.Tech final-year project exploring intelligent quality-aware underwater image and video enhancement. The system demonstrates a complete pipeline: from deep learning classification to physics-guided multi-scale restoration, to quantitative quality measurement — all served via a live Flask web application with a secure REST API.

The UIEB (Underwater Image Enhancement Benchmark) dataset was used for training and evaluation. All three architectures (Custom CNN, MobileNetV2, ResNet-50) were trained from scratch using PyTorch, with MobileNetV2 selected for production based on its 93% accuracy at real-time inference speeds.
